FBI-Recorded Crimes Against Society

The agency's transition to NIBRS in 2021

FBI statistics on the last 10 years' arrests in the crimes against society category show a negative correlation with time.

The Uniform Crime Reporting Program (UCR), which has been around since 1930, is the source of the data.

Its scope includes statistics on arrests for crimes broken down by categories and subcategories, for example the broad category "violent crime" and one subcategory "murder."

While this downward trend could be merely seen as an indicator of dwindling crime or less data existing, there is a third factor at play.

In 2021, the FBI made a switch to only include data from NIBRS aka the National Incident-Based Reporting System.

The Bureau of Justice Statistics mentions some key benefits of this system: "Detailed, high-quality data that provide a more complete and accurate picture of crime" and "Additional context to understand victimization and offending."

This includes more demographic information about people involved in an incident and what type of weapons or property were involved.

Drunkenness arrests numbered in six digits in the sampled data in the years through 2020. Then in 2021, the arrests reported dropped to 299.

Year:

Even lower than drunkenness are suspicion reports, with only seven reported in 2021, and 63 in 2022

The FBI's interactive Crime Data explorer offers a comment not left on other crimes against society, "Suspicion data is not available for 2021 data year due to the transition to NIBRS."


All data reported in this article gathered from FBI API, at https://api.usa.gov/crime/fbi/cde/arrest/national/society
